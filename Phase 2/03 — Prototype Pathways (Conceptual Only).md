## 4. Retrieval Prototype Pathway (Conceptual)

### 4.1 Objective
Determine whether Mars Raccoon can retrieve tubes:

- gently  
- reliably  
- without precision robotics  
- while tolerating dust and misalignment  

---

### 4.2 Conceptual Prototype Strategy
If execution were licensed, practical retrieval prototyping would:

- begin with static bench setups
- test funnel geometries
- validate compliant capture approaches
- measure force thresholds for safe pull
- explore shallow burial tolerance
- validate ‚Äúfail and walk away‚Äù thresholds

Retrieval development is a **geometry + mechanics** problem first, not a ‚Äúrobotics‚Äù problem.

---

### 4.3 Special Retrieval Testing Priorities
Conceptual priorities include:

- misalignment tolerance
- dust surface slickness + adhesion behavior
- unexpected snag resistance
- tube slippage behavior
- abort behavior reliability

If retrieval cannot meet acceptable safety behavior,
Mars Raccoon may still proceed as a **vault + consolidation** asset
but this document assumes retrieval will be pushed as far as honest engineering allows.

## 5. Vault Prototype Pathway (Conceptual)

### 5.1 Objective
Demonstrate that the vault:

- retains tubes reliably  
- protects tubes from impact and vibration  
- remains safe during shock or partial chassis failure  
- requires **no power** to remain secure  

Vault = core system value.
Vault must be validated with near-paranoid confidence.

---

### 5.2 Conceptual Prototype Strategy
If licensed, vault prototyping would:

- start as inert mechanical structures
- validate retention geometry
- test shock + vibration survivability
- test internal cushioning concepts
- test tube separation and collision prevention
- validate dust intrusion resilience at intake interface

Vault prototypes should intentionally receive abuse rather than admiration.

---

### 5.3 Special Vault Priorities
Key conceptual proof cases:

- retention under sudden impact  
- retention under inversion or tilt  
- internal organization integrity  
- intake sealing philosophy resilience  
- performance after simulated long-duration degradation  

Vault success is the most central Phase 2 goal conceptually.

### 6.3 Special Power Priorities

## 7. Mobility & Stability Prototype Pathway (Conceptual)

### 7.1 Objective
Demonstrate that mobility can:

- avoid high-risk maneuvers

- stationary depot survivability evaluations

The goal is not acrobatics ‚Äî it is boring competence.

### 7.3 Special Mobility Priorities

## 8. Integration Maturation (Conceptual)

Once independent conceptual prototypes demonstrate confidence in theory, responsible execution **would** proceed to:

- combine subsystems
- test transition behavior between modes
- characterize degraded states
- validate failure coherence

This should only occur after major risks are already reduced individually.
Integration should confirm maturity, not discover disasters.

---

## 9. Failure-Centric Demonstrations (Conceptual)

Phase 2 should **not** just show success cases.
Responsible engineering would focus heavily on conceptual failure proofing:

- retrieval jams
- partial burial failures
- vault partial shock failures
- mobility stall states
- comms outages
- power starvation
- compound stresses

The correct standard is:

> ‚ÄúFailure was boring, safe, and predictable.‚Äù

> ‚ÄúIt worked beautifully when everything went great.‚Äù
